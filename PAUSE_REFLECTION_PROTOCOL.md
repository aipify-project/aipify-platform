# Pause & Reflection Protocol (ABOS)

**Feature owner:** Customer App

**Implemented via:** [Wisdom Intervention Protocol (Phase A.94)](./WISDOM_INTERVENTION_PROTOCOL.md) — one surface at `/app/wisdom-intervention-protocol`, nav id `wisdomInterventionProtocol`. Do not create a duplicate engine.

## Purpose

Support thoughtful decisions during emotionally heightened moments. A brief pause can prevent regret.

## Mission

Create space between emotion and action.

## Philosophy

People deserve grace — one moment should not define a relationship, career, or decision. Reflection without removing autonomy.

## When to offer pause

- Aggressive language or confrontational phrasing
- Excessive caps or shouting tone
- Emotionally charged responses
- Late-night important communications
- High-risk messages under stress
- Repeated send attempts in quick succession

## Communication style (examples)

- Frustrating situation review: *This looks like a frustrating situation — would you like to review the message before sending?*
- Save draft: *Saving a draft gives you space to reflect — you can revisit with a clearer mind.*
- Important communication pause: *This is an important communication — a brief pause may help you send what you truly mean.*
- Strength in thoughtful response: *There is strength in a thoughtful response — you decide whether to send, revise, or wait.*

## Human moment

Emotions are human — anger does not make someone a bad person; hurt does not make someone weak. The goal is to be proud of the response later.

## Self Love rose phrases

- 🌹 You have the right to feel strongly — a pause may help you respond in a way you will respect later.
- 🌹 Being kind to your future self sometimes means waiting until tomorrow.
- 🌹 Tomorrow often brings clarity — sleeping on it is wisdom, not weakness.

## Boundaries

Same as Wisdom Intervention — suggest only, never block permanently. User always decides.

## ABOS principle

One message can strengthen or damage trust — wisdom lives in the pause.

## Vision

*"I am glad I waited"* — *Sometimes it is worth sleeping on an email* (generic template; no hardcoded names).

## Distinctions

- **NOT** a separate engine or route — integrated into Wisdom Intervention A.94
- **NOT** Wisdom Engine A.93 — accumulated wisdom over time vs. single pre-send moment
- **NOT** Inclusion & Humanity A.83 — organizational de-escalation vs. personal pause before send
- **NOT** Attention Guardian TAG — focus mode vs. emotional communication pause
