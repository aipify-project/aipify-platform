# Resilience and Recovery

Resilience is a pillar of the **Aipify Business Operating System (ABOS)** — not the absence of difficulty, but the ability to recover, adapt, and grow through it.

## Philosophy

Organizations face disruption, uncertainty, and crisis. Resilience means recovering, adapting, and growing through difficulty — not pretending difficulty never happens.

## ABOS principle

> Strength is revealed in difficult moments — navigate with confidence, compassion, and clarity.

## What the Resilience Engine covers

The ABOS Resilience Engine extends **Phase A.50** at `/app/organizational-resilience-engine`:

- **Scenario-based continuity plans** — critical employee absence, support backlog, supplier disruption, integration failure, operational incident, rapid growth
- **Simulations** — tabletop exercises, walkthroughs, recovery reviews, lessons learned
- **Vulnerability tracking** — operational gaps linked to plans with severity and resolution
- **Structured reviews** — scheduled plan reviews with findings metadata and human approval

## Five resilience dimensions

| Dimension | Focus |
|-----------|-------|
| **Operational** | Process continuity, fallback procedures, recovery priorities |
| **Knowledge** | Documented procedures, role clarity, institutional memory |
| **Human** | Team capacity, backup roles, recovery periods |
| **Customer** | Communication during disruption, transparent updates |
| **Strategic** | Priority decisions, adaptation, long-term recovery |

## Crisis support

During disruption, Aipify surfaces relevant information, approved procedures, and clear next steps. **Humans lead crisis response.** Aipify coordinates, recommends, and informs — it does not replace executive decision-making.

## Vision

A steady companion when circumstances are not — rising again, not never falling.
