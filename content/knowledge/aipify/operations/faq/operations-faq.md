The Autonomous Operations Center (AOC) provides visibility into the health and flow of your organization. It acts as an AI Chief of Staff — observing, prioritizing, and recommending without taking control away from humans.

title: What is Operational Health?
slug: what-is-operational-health
category: operations
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [aoc, health, faq]
article_type: faq
---
Operational Health Score (0–100) represents the overall condition of key organizational functions: support, knowledge, quality, governance, action, communication, security, value, and organizational flow (Twin). Bands: Excellent (90+), Healthy (75–89), Attention Recommended (60–74), Operational Risk (40–59), Critical (below 40).

---
title: Can Watchers take actions automatically?
slug: can-watchers-take-actions
category: operations
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [aoc, watchers, faq]
article_type: faq
---
No. Watchers observe and recommend. They detect rising backlogs, knowledge gaps, approval bottlenecks, task congestion, and other signals — but never execute production actions automatically.

---
title: How are recommendations generated?
slug: how-are-aoc-recommendations-generated
category: operations
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [aoc, recommendations, faq]
article_type: faq
---
Recommendations are based on evidence from operational signals, Watcher findings, Digital Twin data, and platform patterns. Each recommendation explains why it exists, supporting evidence, expected benefit, risk level, and suggested next steps.

---
title: What reviews does AOC generate?
slug: what-reviews-does-aoc-generate
category: operations
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [aoc, reviews, faq]

Daily Operations Review (what needs attention today), Weekly Operations Review (trends and health changes), and Executive Operations Review (strategic risks and leadership priorities). All reviews require human interpretation and action.
