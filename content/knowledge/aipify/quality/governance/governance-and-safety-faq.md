Production websites affect real customers and revenue. Quality Guardian is designed to observe and recommend first. Automatic deploys, database changes, and checkout modifications are blocked by default so teams stay in control.

---
title: Why does Aipify ask for approval?
slug: why-does-aipify-ask-for-approval
category: quality-governance
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [quality, governance, approval]
keywords: [approval, governance, trust]
article_type: faq
priority: 74
---
Approval ensures sensitive actions are visible, explained, and logged. Medium-risk actions such as CMS image updates or publishing Knowledge Center articles require admin approval. High-risk actions such as production code changes require explicit developer review.

---
title: What actions are blocked by default?
slug: what-actions-are-blocked-by-default
category: quality-governance
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [quality, governance, blocked]
keywords: [blocked actions, automatic, billing]
article_type: faq
priority: 73
---
Blocked by default: automatic production deploys, automatic database modification, automatic billing or checkout changes, deletion of live assets, and public content replacement without review. Quality Guardian may scan, report, and suggest — not silently change production.

---
title: What happens during Emergency Stop?
slug: what-happens-during-emergency-stop
category: quality-governance
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [quality, governance, emergency stop]
keywords: [emergency stop, governance, pause]
article_type: faq
priority: 72
---
When Emergency Stop is active, Aipify pauses sensitive automated actions across the platform. Quality Guardian continues to observe and record incidents, but recommended fixes that require approval or execution remain blocked until Emergency Stop is lifted.

---
title: Can low-risk fixes be approved?
slug: can-low-risk-fixes-be-approved
category: quality-governance
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [quality, governance, low risk]
keywords: [low risk, approval, recommendations]
article_type: faq
priority: 71
---
Yes. Low-risk recommendations such as draft reports, admin tasks, Knowledge Gap creation, and image compression suggestions can be prepared without production impact. Implementing even low-risk CMS or content changes still follows your governance approval settings.
