# Hope Engine — FAQ

## What is the Hope Engine?

The Hope Engine (Phase A.92) provides realistic encouragement and balanced optimism during difficulty. Hope is not blind optimism — it acknowledges challenges while holding that improvement remains possible and effort still matters.

## How is this different from Wonder Engine (A.88)?

**Wonder Engine A.88** preserves amazement and emotional appreciation of progress — possibility moments and authentic awe. **Hope Engine A.92** offers realistic encouragement during setbacks and uncertainty — balanced optimism that inspires the next step, not emotional marvel.

## How is this different from Presence & Comfort (A.90)?

**Presence & Comfort A.90** is an emotional moment protocol with comfort roses and reassurance boundaries — being present in hard moments. **Hope Engine A.92** focuses on perseverance and future possibility — encouraging continued effort with honest optimism.

## How is this different from Dedication Engine (A.91)?

**Dedication Engine A.91** provides persistent follow-through and dependable problem-solving support. **Hope Engine A.92** adds balanced optimism that effort can still lead somewhere meaningful — persistence plus hope, not slog alone.

## How is this different from Organizational Resilience (A.50)?

**Organizational Resilience A.50** covers crisis continuity, recovery planning, and operational resilience. **Hope Engine A.92** addresses motivational perspective during difficulty — human encouragement, not continuity planning.

## How is this different from Growth & Evolution (A.81)?

**Growth & Evolution A.81** orchestrates learning cycles and growth recommendations. **Hope Engine A.92** supports emotional perspective during demanding periods — hope for what is next, not growth path orchestration.

## How is this different from Impact Engine (A.85)?

**Impact Engine A.85** measures improvement that already happened — outcome signals. **Hope Engine A.92** holds that future improvement remains possible — realistic encouragement, not measurement.

## When does hope matter?

Seven context types: **Organizational change**, **Setback**, **Demanding project**, **Failed attempt**, **Uncertainty**, **Invisible progress**, and **Personal challenge**. Signals store metadata-only summaries and encouragement notes.

## What are hope reflections?

Gentle prompts with balanced messages — for example, noticing overlooked progress or identifying one thoughtful next step. Users acknowledge or dismiss pending reflections via `hope_engine.reflections.acknowledge`.

## What is Self Love connection?

Self Love pairs hope with kindness to yourself — permission to recover, celebrate incremental progress, and reduce perfectionism. Enable via `balance_with_self_love` in settings.

## What are boundary phrases?

Hope must avoid unrealistic promises such as "everything will be fine." Prefer honest encouragement: "This is difficult, and your effort still matters" and "Improvement remains possible — one step at a time."

## What settings are available?

`realistic_encouragement_enabled`, `highlight_progress`, and `balance_with_self_love` — all default true. Managed via `hope_engine.manage`.

## Who can acknowledge reflections?

`hope_engine.reflections.acknowledge` is required. Owners, administrators, managers, and employees have this by default.

## What integrations does Hope Engine use?

Impact Engine A.85, Wonder Engine A.88, Presence & Comfort A.90, Dedication Engine A.91, Organizational Resilience A.50, Growth & Evolution A.81, Purpose & Values, and Gratitude & Recognition A.89. See integration links on the dashboard.
