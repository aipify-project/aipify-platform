# Presence & Comfort Protocol — FAQ

## What is the Presence & Comfort Protocol?

The Presence & Comfort Protocol (Phase A.90) responds during emotionally significant moments — sometimes with reassurance, not solutions. It offers warmth, presence, and respectful support while remaining honest about Aipify's nature.

## How is this different from Gratitude & Recognition (A.89)?

**Gratitude & Recognition A.89** uses Digital Recognition Roses (🌹) for peer appreciation to colleagues. **Presence & Comfort A.90** uses Comfort Roses for care during difficulty — same symbol, different intent. Recognition = noticing effort; Comfort = being present in hard moments.

## How is this different from Companion Presence (A.67)?

**Companion Presence A.67** is the floating orb UI indicator showing Aipify is available. **Presence & Comfort A.90** is the emotional response protocol — what Aipify says and how it supports during vulnerability, exhaustion, or discouragement.

## How is this different from Inclusion & Humanity (A.83)?

**Inclusion & Humanity A.83** governs organizational communication conduct, de-escalation, and inclusion principles. **Presence & Comfort A.90** focuses on individual emotional moments — loneliness, exhaustion, vulnerability — with comfort roses and human connection encouragement.

## How is this different from Humor and Personality?

**Humor/Personality** (`/app/personality`) provides playful humor modes and bell personality seeds. **Presence & Comfort** offers calm, gentle reassurance — never clinical, dismissive, or performative humor during difficulty.

## How is this different from PAME and LifeOS?

**PAME** (`/app/assistant/memory`) stores personal memories with user approval. **LifeOS** (`/app/assistant/life`) manages life priorities and briefings. **Presence & Comfort** does not store life content — metadata-only comfort moment summaries and protocol event counts.

## What are comfort roses?

Comfort roses (🌹) signal care during difficulty: "I am here with you", "Be gentle with yourself", "Thank you for sharing that", "Small steps still count." They are **not** peer recognition roses — they express presence, not colleague appreciation.

## What boundaries does the protocol enforce?

Avoid false human claims: "I know exactly how you feel", "I love you too", "I can't live without you." Prefer honest support: "I am here with you", "That sounds really difficult", "You deserve support from people who care about you."

## What is protocol sensitivity?

`balanced` (default) or `gentle` — controls how proactively comfort responses and human connection prompts are offered. Gentle mode favors softer pacing and fewer suggestions.

## Who can manage settings and record moments?

`presence_comfort.manage` is required for settings updates and `record_comfort_rose_moment`. Owners, administrators, and managers have this by default.

## What data is stored?

Metadata only — comfort message summaries (max 500 chars), moment types, rose_used flags, and aggregate protocol event counts. No raw chat, emails, or PII.

## What integrations does Presence & Comfort use?

Gratitude & Recognition A.89, Companion Identity A.84, Inclusion & Humanity A.83, Personality, Wonder Engine A.88, and Purpose & Values A.82. See integration links on the dashboard.
