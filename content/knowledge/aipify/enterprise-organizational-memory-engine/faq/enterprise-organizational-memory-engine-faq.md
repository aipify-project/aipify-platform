# Enterprise Memory, Organizational Knowledge & Collective Intelligence Engine — FAQ

## What is Organizational Memory?

Organizational Memory at `/app/knowledge/memory` captures institutional knowledge — policies, decisions, processes, and operational learnings — so teams can retrieve what the organization already knows without repeating discovery.

## How is knowledge captured?

Knowledge assets, decision records, and process memory are added through governed actions. Sources connect to approved ingestion paths. Low-confidence or unvalidated knowledge can be flagged for review before broad use.

## How does this differ from the Learning Engine?

**Learning Engine** (`/app/learning`) improves how Aipify behaves for your organization with your approval. **Organizational Memory** stores what the organization knows — decisions, policies, and institutional knowledge for people and Aipify to reference.

## How does this relate to Knowledge Center?

**Knowledge Center** provides articles and guides. **Organizational Memory** stores tenant-specific institutional knowledge — your decisions, policies, and operational memory — with governance and retention controls.

## How is knowledge validated?

Validation actions mark knowledge as reviewed and approved. Governance routes ensure owners and admins control what becomes organizational memory. Audit logs record capture, validation, and access events.
