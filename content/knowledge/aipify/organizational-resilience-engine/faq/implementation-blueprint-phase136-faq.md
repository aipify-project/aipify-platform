# Implementation Blueprint Phase 136 — Self-Healing Operations & Organizational Recovery Engine FAQ

## What is Autonomous Organization Phase 136?

Phase 136 extends the Organizational Resilience Engine (Phase A.50 / ABOS Resilience Engine) with Blueprint Phase 81, Phase 91, and Phase 128 with a **Self-Healing Operations & Organizational Recovery** layer — operational health monitoring, recovery detection, incident learning, and recovery orchestration with **transparent governed recovery and no hidden actions**. It layers on the same route `/app/organizational-resilience-engine` — no duplicate recovery center.

## How is Phase 136 different from Phase 128?

**Phase 128** (`_rccbp128_*`) = continuity companion — prepare/adapt/recover with readiness not command. **Phase 136** (`_shobp136_*`) = self-healing operations — detect operational degradation early, coordinate transparent recovery, capture lessons learned, and strengthen organizations beyond return to normal. All phases share A.50 resilience metadata.

## How is Phase 136 different from Incident Response A.51 and Continuity Phase 80?

**Incident Response A.51** at `/app/incident-response-coordination-engine` handles coordinated incident response with ownership and escalation — cross-link only. **Continuity Phase 80** at `/app/continuity` handles crisis continuity — backup ownership, incident mode. **Phase 136** focuses on **organizational self-healing operations** — governed recovery recommendations, incident learning metadata, and recovery orchestration with human accountability.

## How is Phase 136 different from platform self-healing?

**Platform `ai_self_healing_executions`** is the technical automation layer for approved low-risk repairs. **Phase 136** is the **organizational recovery layer** — transparent degradation detection, human-approved recommendations, lessons learned, and executive visibility. No high-risk automatic fixes.

## What is the Self-Healing Operations Center?

Eight capabilities: operational health monitoring, incident coordination cross-link, recovery recommendations, knowledge restoration, companion recovery support, escalation workflows, lessons learned, and executive dashboards.

## What does the recovery detection engine monitor?

Seven aggregate signals: repeated workflow failures, companion escalation surges, knowledge interruptions, support backlogs, governance exceptions, communication breakdowns, and executive overload indicators — systemic metadata only, never individual blame scoring.

## What are companion limitations?

No autonomous governance changes · no concealing incidents · no overriding leadership · no independent high-risk execution · no suppressing dissent. The Recovery Companion is **supportive not directive**.

## What are the Phase 136 success criteria?

Computed live via `_shobp136_success_criteria()` — objectives, Self-Healing Operations Center, operational health engine, recovery detection, self-healing framework, Recovery Companion, incident learning, recovery orchestration, healing principles, limitations, security requirements, cross-links, success metrics, Phase 128 field preservation, and operational recovery workflow validation.
