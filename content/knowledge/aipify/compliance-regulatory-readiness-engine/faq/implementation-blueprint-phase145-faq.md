# Global Compliance & Regulatory Intelligence — Phase 145 FAQ

> **Important:** This FAQ describes Aipify product capabilities for educational support and preparedness. It is **not legal advice**. Consult qualified legal and compliance professionals for regulatory guidance.

## FAQ 1

**Question:** What is Phase 145 Global Compliance & Regulatory Intelligence?

**Answer:** Phase 145 extends the Compliance & Regulatory Readiness Engine (A.29) at `/app/compliance-regulatory-readiness-engine` with Global Intelligence Era depth — regulatory awareness scaffolds, policy management, compliance review cycles, audit readiness tracking, and Compliance Companion guidance. It does not create a duplicate compliance center.

## FAQ 2

**Question:** Does Aipify provide legal advice?

**Answer:** No. Aipify provides educational support and preparedness guidance only. The Compliance Companion helps with policy navigation, checklists, and documentation scaffolds — it does not interpret regulations as law or guarantee compliance outcomes. Consult qualified professionals for legal advice.

## FAQ 3

**Question:** How is Phase 145 different from Phase 144 Global Governance & Diplomacy?

**Answer:** Phase 144 at `/app/global-governance-diplomacy-engine` focuses on global governance and diplomatic coordination across the ecosystem. Phase 145 focuses on organization-level compliance preparedness — documentation, reviews, policies, and audit readiness. They cross-link but serve different purposes.

## FAQ 4

**Question:** What are compliance review cycles?

**Answer:** Review cycles track scheduled compliance activities such as policy reviews, documentation reviews, control verification, companion governance reviews, executive attestations, readiness assessments, and gap identification. Executives can record attestations with full audit trail.

## FAQ 5

**Question:** What is the policy registry?

**Answer:** The policy registry stores metadata about internal policies, security standards, companion usage policies, governance guidelines, and operations procedures. It tracks ownership, status, and review dates — not the full legal text of policies.

## FAQ 6

**Question:** What are audit readiness items?

**Answer:** Audit readiness items are evidence tracking scaffolds — documentation preparation, control tracking, review histories, and audit coordination metadata. They help organizations prepare for audits without storing sensitive operational records.

## FAQ 7

**Question:** How does Phase 145 relate to Records Retention (A.60)?

**Answer:** A.29 retention policies and A.60 Records Retention Management cross-link for data lifecycle governance. Phase 145 adds policy registry and review cycle depth — it does not duplicate retention engine RPCs.

## FAQ 8

**Question:** What can the Compliance Companion do?

**Answer:** The Compliance Companion may retrieve compliance knowledge, navigate policies, prepare checklists, coordinate reviews, summarize readiness status, and highlight gap findings. It must not provide legal opinions, guarantee outcomes, replace professionals, or override executive accountability.

## FAQ 9

**Question:** What Growth Partner compliance support is included?

**Answer:** Growth Partner compliance support covers implementation standards, customer documentation expectations, governance alignment, security practices, professional conduct, and certification requirements — cross-linking `/app/partners` and Trust Network Phase 142.

## FAQ 10

**Question:** Is compliance data private to my organization?

**Answer:** Yes. All compliance data is organization-scoped with RBAC access controls. Metadata only — no raw operational records. Policy changes, reviews, and attestations are auditable. 2FA is recommended for compliance administrators.
