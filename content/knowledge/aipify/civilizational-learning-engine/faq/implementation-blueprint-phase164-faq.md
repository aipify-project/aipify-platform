# Civilizational Learning & Collective Adaptation Engine — FAQ

## What is the Collective Adaptation Center?

The Collective Adaptation Center at `/app/civilizational-learning-engine` is part of the **Post-Enterprise & Civilizational Era (161–170)**. It supports shared learning and adaptive intelligence through humility and reflection — **not** centralized authority or universal consensus.

## How is Phase 164 different from Phase 163 (Civilizational Memory)?

Civilizational Memory (`/app/civilizational-memory-engine`) focuses on **preservation with discernment** — what institutional wisdom to safeguard. Phase 164 focuses on **collective adaptation and learning** — how organizations evolve through shared reflection. Cross-link only; **do not duplicate** Phase 163 RPCs.

## How is Phase 164 different from Phase 141 (Global Knowledge Exchange)?

Global Knowledge Exchange (`/app/global-knowledge-exchange-engine`) enables **voluntary interorganizational knowledge exchange**. Phase 164 is a **tenant collective adaptation center** for learning programs, adaptation reviews, and lessons learned libraries. Cross-link only; **do not duplicate** Phase 141 RPCs.

## How is Phase 164 different from Continuous Improvement (Phase 134)?

Continuous Improvement (`/app/continuous-improvement-engine`) handles **org-level improvement loops**. Phase 164 addresses **civilizational-era collective learning** — cross-generational programs, executive learning reviews, and humility-based adaptation. Cross-link only.

## Does the Adaptation Companion determine priorities?

**No.** The Adaptation Companion supports growth with learning summaries, reflection prompts, and preparedness briefings. It does **not** determine priorities, suppress perspectives, replace leadership accountability, claim predictive certainty, or override governance.

## Is cross-organizational learning mandatory?

**No.** New tenants default to **opt-out** (`cross_org_learning_opt_in = false`). Organizations must explicitly enable cross-org learning scaffolds.

**Metadata summaries only** — program scaffolds, adaptation review records, and lessons learned library entries (max 500 characters per summary). No surveillance. No organizational ranking.
