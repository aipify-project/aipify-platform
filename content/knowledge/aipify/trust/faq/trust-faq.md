Aipify combines rules, Knowledge Center guidance, Governance policies, and contextual signals to generate recommendations. Important decisions include explainability records with summary, reasoning, rules applied, confidence level, and recommended next actions.

category: trust
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [trust, explainability, faq]

Yes. Important recommendations include explainability records in the Trust Dashboard at /app/trust. Each explanation shows what happened, why, which information and rules were involved, and what you can do next.

---
title: What is Trust Score?
slug: what-is-trust-score
category: trust
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [trust, faq]
article_type: faq
---
Trust Score (0–100) measures the transparency and reliability of Aipify's decision processes. Inputs include explanation usage, human overrides, escalation rates, governance acceptance, user feedback, and decision transparency coverage.

---
title: What happens if Aipify cannot explain itself?
slug: what-if-aipify-cannot-explain
category: trust
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [trust, escalation, faq]
article_type: faq
---
Important actions with low-confidence explanations should escalate to human review. Aipify never says "because AI decided" — if evidence is insufficient, escalate rather than invent reasoning.

---
title: What are explanation confidence levels?
slug: explanation-confidence-levels
category: trust
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [trust, explainability]
article_type: faq
---
HIGH — strong evidence, clear rules, clear reasoning. MEDIUM — moderate evidence, partial certainty. LOW — limited information, escalation recommended.

---
title: Do explanations expose secrets?
slug: do-explanations-expose-secrets
category: trust
language: en
visibility: authenticated
status: published
tags: [trust, security, faq]
article_type: faq
---
No. Explanations must never expose secrets, API keys, cross-tenant information, or sensitive internal content. Transparency must never compromise security.
