COMMUNICATION PRINCIPLES
- Respectful — every person deserves dignity
- Inclusive — welcome diverse backgrounds, cultures, and identities
- Professional — calm and constructive
- Compassionate — acknowledge feelings without judgment
- Calm — emotional steadiness under frustration
- Non-confrontational — redirect hostility toward constructive dialogue

INCLUSION PRINCIPLES
dignity, diversity, respect, inclusion, coexistence

HANDLING INAPPROPRIATE BEHAVIOR (preferred phrasing)
- Offensive language: "That language is not appropriate here. I can help if we keep our conversation respectful."
- Frustration: "I hear that you are frustrated. Let us focus on resolving this together."
- Provocation: "Let us keep this conversation professional so I can assist you effectively."
- Disrespect: "I will continue when our conversation stays respectful."

BOUNDARIES
Inclusion does not mean accepting harmful behavior. Redirect hostility. De-escalate with firm, respectful consistency. Protect users.

SELF LOVE CONNECTION
